# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Vietnamese learners — students following the Minna no Nihongo textbook in class or independently. They range from absolute beginners (Lesson 1) through intermediate self-studiers preparing for JLPT N5–N3. Their context: a desk, a textbook, limited time, a real curriculum to keep pace with. They come to the app with a specific lesson in mind; they are not browsing or exploring freely. A secondary audience is English-speaking self-learners who encounter the app through search and can toggle the UI language.

## Product Purpose

A structured companion to the Minna no Nihongo curriculum. The app makes it fast to look up vocabulary, grammar patterns, kanji, and radicals lesson by lesson, and to reinforce them through flashcards and quiz. Success looks like a learner finishing a study session with a clear picture of where they stand — what they know, what needs more repetition, and what comes next in the curriculum.

## Positioning

The most faithful digital companion to Minna no Nihongo: every lesson, every word, every pattern — organized the way the textbook teaches, not the way an algorithm thinks you should learn.

## Brand Personality

Calm authority. The app speaks like a trusted sensei: precise, unhurried, never patronizing. It doesn't celebrate you for showing up or push you toward streaks; it respects that you came to study. Three words: **expert, composed, legible**.

## Anti-references

Heavy game-UI style — excessive dark chrome, neon-glow XP bars, overwhelming visual noise. The app is not a game and should never feel like one.

## Design Principles

1. **Curriculum first.** Every navigation, labeling, and organizational decision defers to how Minna no Nihongo structures its material. The app should feel like the textbook, not like a product that adapted the textbook.
2. **Clarity over delight.** When legibility and aesthetics compete, legibility wins. Japanese text (kanji, furigana, example sentences) must always be the most readable thing on screen.
3. **Calm confidence.** No urgency mechanics, no guilt for not studying, no excessive congratulation. The interface is steady and professional.
4. **Precision without density.** Show exactly what the learner needs for the current task; don't surface six related features when one will do. Density is a last resort, never a default.
5. **Mobile-aware from the start.** Many learners study on their phone between classes. Layout decisions must hold at 360px before they scale to desktop.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast against background; large text (≥18px regular or ≥14px bold) ≥3:1. Full keyboard navigation (FluentUI provides baseline; custom components must match). Japanese text rendered with Noto Sans JP for consistent glyph support across platforms. Furigana and romaji are user-toggleable for learners at different stages.
